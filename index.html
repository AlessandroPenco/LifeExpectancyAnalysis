Life Expectancy Analysis

Introduction

Life expectancy is a critical metric used to determine the average length of time a person is expected to live. The value of this metric varies widely across the world, with different countries and regions experiencing different trends in life expectancy. In developed nations, life expectancy is generally higher due to factors such as access to healthcare, better nutrition, and overall higher standards of living. However, in developing nations, life expectancy can be much lower, with many factors such as disease, poor nutrition, and lack of access to healthcare playing a role.

Map chart

This plot displays the median age for each state on a map. The median age or life expectancy median can be useful in several ways. Firstly, it provides a measure of central tendency of the life expectancy of a population, which can be helpful in better understanding the distribution of the data.

For instance, if the median life expectancy of a population is much lower than the mean, this may suggest that there are some parts of the population that live much shorter lives than the majority. Moreover, the median can be useful in comparing life expectancy between different populations or groups.

For example, comparing the median life expectancy between countries with different levels of economic development could provide insights into inequalities in access to healthcare and medical assistance. Lastly, the value of the median life expectancy can be used to make predictions about future needs of healthcare and pension systems.

For instance, if the median life expectancy is increasing in a particular geographical area, this may suggest that there will be greater needs for healthcare and social protection for the elderly in the future.

Radar (Spider) chart

The Radar chart is a comprehensive visualization that combines several data points seen in previous visualizations. It allows us to relate total life expectancy, male and female life expectancy, GDP, and healthcare spending.

This enables us to visualize whether a higher standard of living and consequent higher healthcare spending allows the population to have a longer life expectancy. The chart allows us to select the year to analyze through the main slider, and through the legend, we can choose to highlight the visualization of a specific continent.

Through the chart, we can observe that there is a correlation between a higher life expectancy and a higher per capita healthcare spending.

However, as expected, the extension of life expectancy is not proportional to healthcare spending. For example, Europe, which invests significantly higher amounts in healthcare than other continents, has only slightly higher life expectancy.
