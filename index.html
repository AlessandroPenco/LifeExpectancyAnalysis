Life Expectancy Analysis

Map chart

Life expectancy is a critical metric used to determine the average length of time a person is expected to live. The value of this metric varies widely across the world, with different countries and regions experiencing different trends in life expectancy. In developed nations, life expectancy is generally higher due to factors such as access to healthcare, better nutrition, and overall higher standards of living. However, in developing nations, life expectancy can be much lower, with many factors such as disease, poor nutrition, and lack of access to healthcare playing a role.

A map chart is an effective way to visualize life expectancy across the world. This type of chart displays data by assigning different colors or shades to different regions of the world. The resulting image can be used to quickly see trends in life expectancy across different countries and regions. For example, a map chart can show that life expectancy is higher in developed regions such as Europe, North America, and parts of Asia, while it is lower in regions such as Africa and South Asia. This type of chart can also be useful in identifying countries or regions where life expectancy is lower than expected, which can help policymakers and healthcare professionals to develop targeted interventions to improve health outcomes.

Line chart

In addition to a map chart, a subsequent line chart that displays the life expectancy during the years for each continent and the world as a whole can provide a deeper understanding of life expectancy trends over time. By using a line chart, we can see how life expectancy has changed in each continent and the world as a whole over a period of time, giving us a better understanding of how healthcare interventions and other factors have affected life expectancy in different regions of the world.

For example, a line chart could show that life expectancy has steadily increased over the past few decades in developed regions such as Europe and North America, with a more gradual increase in other regions such as Asia and Latin America. It could also show that there has been a significant increase in life expectancy in certain countries in Africa in recent years, due to improved healthcare and increased access to medicines.

Bar chart

A subsequent bar chart that displays the life expectancy during the years (between 1960 and 2018) for each continent can provide even further insights into life expectancy trends. By comparing life expectancy between different continents and between men and women, we can gain a deeper understanding of the factors that contribute to life expectancy differences.

The bar chart highlight the impact of healthcare interventions on life expectancy, by showing the difference in life expectancy between earlier years and more recent years. For instance, we could see that life expectancy has improved significantly in certain regions in Africa in recent years due to improved healthcare, but there is still a significant gap between developed and developing regions.

Bubble chart

A subsequent bubble chart that displays the relation between GDP per capita and life expectancy for each country can provide further insights into the relationship between economic development and health outcomes. This type of chart can help us to understand how improvements in economic development can lead to better health outcomes and vice versa.

The bubble chart is grouped by continent and for each year, allowing us to see how the relationship between GDP per capita and life expectancy has changed over time in different regions of the world. Ee could see that in some regions, such as Europe and North America, there is a strong positive correlation between GDP per capita and life expectancy, while in other regions, such as Africa, the correlation is weaker.

The size of the bubbles in the chart represent the size of each country's population, allowing us to see which countries have the most significant impact on global trends. We could also use color coding to represent the different continents.

Box chart

A subsequent box chart that displays life expectancy grouped by continent and for each year can provide even more detailed insights into the distribution of life expectancy across different regions of the world. Box charts are especially useful for showing the range and distribution of a dataset, allowing us to see how life expectancy varies across different countries and regions.

The box chart is used to identify outliers, which are countries that have significantly higher or lower life expectancy than the rest of the region. This information can be used to inform policy decisions and interventions, such as identifying countries that might benefit from additional healthcare resources or public health campaigns.
